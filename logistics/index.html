Lijiang Astronomical Observation Base (Southern Base)

The Southern Base of Yunnan Observatories (CAS), located in Lijiang, includes the Gaomeigu Observation Area (3,200m altitude) and Yulong County. As a key optical astronomy hub in southern China, it benefits from low latitude, high altitude, and minimal light pollution, achieving 2,000 annual observation hours. The base hosts over 20 telescopes, including China's first 2.4-meter optical telescope (2008) and the 1.8-meter collaborative telescope.

Notable achievements include discovering a bright early-universe quasar (redshift 6.3), identifying 300+ supernovae, and publishing hundreds of studies on black holes and stellar evolution.

Google service

Google service is not available in mainland China without a valid VPN. If your life and work heavily rely on google products (email, map, etc), please identify and set up a good VPN service before arrival. You may also find some local alternatives (e.g., Wechat ,Baidu map, etc) if needed.

Payment Methods in China

Credit cards may not be accepted in many occasions, especially taxi and grocery stores. Therefore, we strongly encourage you to bring some cash with you. Alternatively, you may try to set up mobile payment on your phone, as it is very popular in China and can cover almost all the occasions you may encounter. There are two app payment giants in China, Tencent's WeChat and Alibaba's Alipay, with the latter perhaps more friendly to foreign visitors. Through Alipay Tour Pass, you can easily set up mobile payment and pay through your phone.
